# Canada's National AI Strategy: AI for All — Interactive Learning Course

An interactive, self-paced learning course built from the Government of Canada's 2026 National Artificial Intelligence Strategy document. The course transforms the 50-page federal policy paper into structured modules with quizzes, exercises, and teach-back prompts — designed for public servants, policy professionals, business leaders, educators, and anyone who wants to understand Canada's AI direction.

## How to Use the Interactive App

1. **Open** `Canadas_AI_Strategy_Course.html` in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari).
2. **Navigate** using the sidebar module list. Each module contains an overview, key ideas, examples, exercises, a quiz, and a teach-back prompt.
3. **Customize** your experience using the settings panel (gear icon):
   - Choose from five visual themes: Control Room, Blueprint, Parchment, Terminal, Midnight
   - Enable text-to-speech with adjustable voice, speed, and pitch
   - Add background music via YouTube URL or local audio file
4. **Track progress** — completed modules and quiz scores are saved in your browser.
5. **Earn a certificate** — pass all quizzes with an average score of 70% or higher to generate a downloadable completion certificate.
6. **Ask questions** — use the built-in chatbot to ask content-grounded questions about any module.

No internet connection is required after the initial file download (except for YouTube background music and text-to-speech, which use browser APIs).

## Each Module Contains

- **Overview** — 2–4 paragraphs explaining the topic in plain language
- **Key Ideas** — 4–8 terms with concise definitions, designed to be flashcard-ready
- **Examples** — one general real-world scenario and one professional domain spotlight
- **Exercises** — hands-on activities with step-by-step instructions, time estimates, and difficulty levels
- **Quiz** — 5–10 multiple-choice questions with detailed explanations for every answer
- **Teach-Back** — a synthesis prompt that asks you to explain the concept to a specific audience under a specific constraint

## Key Numbers from the Strategy

These figures appear throughout the course and are useful reference points:

- **12% → 60%** — Current business AI adoption rate → 2034 target
- **$200 billion** — Projected GDP gains from AI-driven productivity (3% increase)
- **250,000** — New jobs projected through AI adoption by 2031
- **90,000** — AI-related job placements for young Canadians
- **$500M** — Canadian Tech Growth Fund for scaling AI companies
- **$500M** — Regional AI Initiative through Regional Development Agencies
- **$500M** — BDC LIFT program for SME AI financing
- **$700M** — Compute Access Fund for sovereign compute for SMEs
- **$200M** — First AI Mission (healthcare)
- **850 MW → 2.3 GW** — Sovereign compute capacity targets by 2030
- **83%** — Share of Canada's electricity from renewable/low-emission sources
